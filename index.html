I'm an Assistant Professor at the Rutgers Department of Linguistics. I'm primarily interested in computational and mathematical approaches to phonological theory.

Research interests:

  • formal language theory
  • learning/grammatical inference
  • model theory/logic
  • representation
  • tone/pitch accent
  • long-distance processes
  • Bantu
  • Japonic
  • Austronesian

Recent activities:

  • Deen Mamadou and I presented at NELS 52 @ Rutgers!
  • Jane Chandlee and I have a paper out in Language on applying recursive program schemes to phonological analysis.
  • I appeared on an episode of the Innovation Heroes podcast on artificial intelligence and language learning.
  • I was on the organizing committee for the 2020/2021 International Conference on Grammatical Inference, and presented a paper there with Wenyue Hua on learning underlying representations. Check out the proceedings!
  • I gave a talk at the Princeton Phonology Forum; here are the slides.
