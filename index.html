I'm an Assistant Professor at the Rutgers Department of Linguistics. I'm primarily interested in computational and mathematical approaches to phonological theory.

Research interests:

  • formal language theory
  • learning/grammatical inference
  • model theory/logic
  • representation
  • tone/pitch accent
  • long-distance processes
  • Bantu
  • Japonic
  • Austronesian

Recent activities:

  • Jane Chandlee & I have finished a manuscript on recursive schemes for phonological analysis that I'm very excited about. Also have papers coming out in LI (with Nick Danis and Luca Iacoponi) and NLLT.
  • Students Wenyue Hua, Huteng Dai, and Shiori Ikawa presented our research (sometimes electronically) at WCCFL, GLOW, and PLC.
  • I gave a talk on computation and tone at Haverford College. Here are the slides.
  • I'm on the organizing committee for the 2020 International Conference on Grammatical Inference, to be held in New York City in August.
