I'm an Assistant Professor at the Rutgers Department of Linguistics. I'm primarily interested in computational and mathematical approaches to phonological theory.

Research interests:

  • formal language theory
  • learning/grammatical inference
  • model theory/logic
  • representation
  • tone/pitch accent
  • long-distance processes
  • Bantu
  • Japonic
  • Austronesian

Recent activities:

  • I've appeared on an episode of the Innovation Heroes podcast on artificial intelligence and language learning.
  • I'm on the organizing committee for the 2020/2021 International Conference on Grammatical Inference, to be held from August 23-27, 2021 online. I'll also be presenting a paper there with Wenyue Hua on learning underlying representations.
  • I gave a talk at the Princeton Phonology Forum; here are the slides.
  • Jane Chandlee & I have a paper on local autosegmental processes out in Glossa and a manuscript on recursive schemes for phonological analysis that has been accepted to appear in Language.
  • Being a dad (!!!).
